# Investments

The Investments page pulls positions from any brokerage, IRA, 401(k), or robo-advisor you've linked. It's a snapshot of what you own — quantity, market value, cost basis, and unrealized gain or loss — not a trading interface.

## What you'll see

- **Portfolio value** — the total market value of every position across linked investment accounts, valued at the most recent price your institution reported.
- **Cost basis** — what you paid for those positions. Only shown when every position reports a basis; some institutions (especially old 401(k)s) don't.
- **Unrealized gain/loss** — value minus cost basis. Green when positive, red when negative.
- **Allocation** — a breakdown by security type (equity, ETF, mutual fund, fixed income, cash, etc.) so you can see the shape of the portfolio at a glance.
- **Holdings** — grouped by account, sorted by value. Symbol, name, quantity (with fractional shares), value, and per-position gain/loss.

## How it stays in sync

Holdings refresh automatically:

- On the initial link of a brokerage account.
- When your institution reports new prices or trades — Plaid sends a webhook and we re-pull holdings.
- On any manual sync from the Accounts page.

Holdings are a *snapshot*, not a transaction feed. Each sync replaces the previous set for that account, so a fully liquidated position simply disappears.

## What it can and can't do

- ✅ Tracks current value, allocation, gain/loss.
- ✅ Counts toward your total net worth as an asset.
- ✅ Respects household sharing — only investment accounts marked as shared appear in household scope.
- 🚫 No day-over-day change yet (we don't store historical prices). Coming later.
- 🚫 No trade history, dividend tracking, or tax-lot detail. Use your brokerage for those.
- 🚫 No buy/sell — Fincenzo is read-only.

## If something looks wrong

- **No holdings showing** — make sure the linked institution actually exposes investments. Many credit unions and neobanks don't. Check the Accounts page for the account type.
- **Cost basis is blank** — your institution didn't report it. Nothing you can do from our side; the gain/loss column will show "—" for those positions.
- **Stale prices** — most institutions report close prices once a day. Intraday prices aren't part of Plaid's investments product.
