# Households

A **household** is a shared layer on top of two personal accounts. Each of you signs in separately with your own credentials, links your own banks, sees your own data. A household lets you opt into sharing specific accounts and budget categories, while everything else stays private to whoever owns it.

The model is per-account, opt-in, and reversible. There is no "share everything" mode. There never will be.

## Creating a household

1. Open the **Household** page in the sidebar.
2. Click **Create household** and give it a name (e.g. "Smith household").
3. You're now the **owner**. You're the only person in it.

## Inviting a partner

From the same page:

1. Click **Invite member** and enter your partner's email address.
2. They get an invite that's visible at the top of their own Fincenzo dashboard the next time they log in (as a banner).
3. They click **Accept** to join, or **Decline** to dismiss.

Invites expire after a set period (currently 7 days). You can revoke a pending invite from the household page.

<Callout type="note">
  Your partner needs their own Fincenzo account first. If they're not signed up yet, ask them to [join the waitlist](/#waitlist) — once they're in, they can accept the invite.
</Callout>

## Sharing accounts

Once you're both in a household, open **Accounts**. Each account row has a **share toggle**:

- **Private** (default) — only you see this account. It's in your dashboard, your budgets, your net worth.
- **Shared** — appears in the household view for both of you.

Sharing is per-account. You can share your joint checking but keep your personal credit card private, while your partner shares their joint checking but keeps their brokerage private.

The household view shows **only the shared accounts**. Your private accounts are invisible to your partner there. Your partner's private accounts are invisible to you.

## Shared budgets

Budgets can also be shared. When creating a budget on the **Budget** page, choose:

- **Personal scope** — only counts your transactions toward this budget. Default.
- **Shared scope** — counts transactions from any shared account, regardless of who linked them. Visible to both of you.

A shared "Groceries" budget will tally groceries spent on your joint checking and your partner's joint checking together, against one shared cap.

## The scope switcher

When you're in a household, the top right of the app shows a **scope switcher** that toggles between:

- **Personal** — what you see when you're solo. Includes all your accounts (private + shared).
- **Household** — only shared accounts and shared budgets, computed for both members.

Every page (dashboard, budgets, transactions, net worth) respects the active scope. The switcher persists across sessions.

## Permissions inside a household

- **Owner** (whoever created it) can invite, remove members, rename, and delete the household.
- **Members** can leave the household at any time.
- **Either of you** can share/unshare your own accounts and edit shared budgets.
- **Neither of you** can unshare or modify the other person's accounts.

You only ever control your own data. Your partner only ever controls theirs. The shared layer is what you've both agreed to combine.

## Leaving a household

To leave, open **Household** and click **Leave household**.

When you leave:

- Your previously shared accounts revert to **private** immediately.
- Your partner stops seeing those accounts in the household view that same second.
- Shared budgets remain in the household with the remaining member; your share of the spend stops contributing.
- Your transaction history, budgets, goals, and net worth come with you, untouched.

If the **owner** leaves, the household dissolves and the remaining member is moved to "no household".

## Activity log

The Household page includes a log of recent activity: joins, leaves, invites issued, accounts shared/unshared. It's there so neither of you can quietly change the sharing posture without the other noticing.

## What a partner never sees

Even with the most permissive sharing setup, your partner cannot see:

- Accounts you haven't explicitly shared.
- Transactions in accounts you haven't shared.
- Personal budgets, personal goals, or your personal net worth.
- Your account password, your email, or your auth session.

Sharing only ever opts an account in. It never opts you into any default beyond that.
