# Connecting accounts

Fincenzo doesn't connect to your bank directly. It uses **Plaid**, the same service most modern money apps use, to do the actual handshake. Your bank credentials go straight to Plaid on a Plaid-hosted screen — Fincenzo never sees them.

## How it works

1. From the **Accounts** page, click **Link an institution**.
2. A Plaid modal opens. Search for your bank and pick it.
3. Log in on Plaid's screen. If your bank uses MFA, you'll do that too.
4. Plaid asks which accounts you want to share. Pick any/all of them.
5. Plaid returns to Fincenzo with an access token. We encrypt it (AES-256-GCM) and store it. The first sync starts in the background within seconds.

You can repeat this for as many institutions as you like — checking at one bank, credit card at another, brokerage at a third.

## What gets synced

For each linked account:

- **Balances** — current and available, refreshed on Plaid's schedule (usually a few times a day).
- **Transactions** — Plaid backfills roughly the last 90 days on first link, then keeps it current via webhooks.
- **Account metadata** — masked number (e.g. `••1234`), name, type, subtype, currency.

Investment accounts also pull **holdings** when Plaid supports it for that institution.

<Callout type="note">
  The very first transaction sync can take a minute or two — Plaid pulls 90 days of history. Subsequent updates land within seconds of your bank posting them.
</Callout>

## Which banks work today

Plaid supports thousands of US institutions. **In the current beta, only non-OAuth institutions work in production.** This is a Plaid-side requirement, not a Fincenzo one.

That covers most credit unions, community banks, neobanks, and quite a few major institutions. The notable exceptions that **don't work yet** are the OAuth-only banks:

- Chase
- Capital One
- Wells Fargo
- Bank of America
- US Bank
- Citi
- USAA
- Fidelity

Adding OAuth support is on the [roadmap](/docs/developers/roadmap) but isn't shipped. If your bank is on this list, you can still try linking — Plaid will tell you OAuth is required and the link will fail gracefully.

## Re-authentication

Banks periodically expire their authorization for security reasons. When that happens:

1. The affected account shows an **"action required"** badge on the Accounts page.
2. Click it to re-open Plaid Link in **update mode** — you only re-enter your credentials, you don't re-pick accounts.
3. Once Plaid hands back a fresh token, syncing resumes automatically.

Most banks force re-auth every 90 days or so. Some (e.g. Capital One via OAuth) require it more often.

## Unlinking

To stop syncing an institution entirely, open **Accounts**, find the institution, and click **Unlink**.

This:

- Tells Plaid to revoke the access token (no more webhooks for this item).
- Marks every account from that institution as unlinked in Fincenzo.
- **Keeps your historical transactions** so charts and budgets don't lose data.

If you want the transactions gone too, delete your account from **Settings → Account → Delete account** (see [Privacy & data](/docs/privacy)).

## Troubleshooting

### "Couldn't link" or the modal closes immediately

Almost always a Plaid-side error. Refresh the page and try again. If it persists, check the browser console — Plaid surfaces a specific error code there.

### Transactions look stuck / out of date

Open the account on the **Accounts** page and click **Sync now**. This bypasses the webhook and asks Plaid for a fresh batch immediately. If the sync still doesn't move, your bank may have expired the token — look for the action-required badge.

### Wrong category on a transaction

That's a categorization concern, not a sync one — see [Categorization & rules](/docs/categorization).

### Duplicate transactions

Plaid occasionally double-posts a pending transaction once it clears. Fincenzo deduplicates by Plaid's `transaction_id`, so the duplicate should resolve within a sync or two. If a duplicate persists for more than a day, [open an issue](https://github.com/xvrgill/fincenzo/issues).
