# Goals

A goal is a **named savings target** with a current value, a target value, and (optionally) a target date. You set them on the Goals page; progress is tracked in dollars.

## Creating a goal

Open **Goals** and click **Add goal**:

- **Name** — what you're saving for ("Emergency fund", "Wedding", "Down payment").
- **Target** — the dollar amount you're aiming for.
- **Current** — what you have toward the goal right now. You can pre-populate this with whatever you've already saved.
- **Target date** (optional) — when you want to hit the target. Used to compute the monthly contribution needed to make it.

Save and a progress ring appears.

## Updating progress

Goals don't auto-sync with account balances — they're explicitly user-managed. When you move money toward a goal, open it and bump the "current" value. The progress ring fills.

This is deliberate. Tying a goal to a single account ("my Ally savings") fails the moment you spread the savings across multiple accounts, sell some assets toward it, or borrow from it temporarily. Manual updates stay honest in those situations.

<Callout type="tip">
  Set a recurring calendar reminder (1st of the month works well) to update goal balances. Five minutes, end-to-end.
</Callout>

## Personal vs. shared goals

If you're in a household, you can create a goal as **Shared**. Shared goals appear on both your and your partner's Goals page, and either of you can update them.

Private goals are invisible to your partner, period. Your partner has their own private goals you can't see either.

## Deleting a goal

Open the goal and click **Delete**. There's no archive — deleted goals are gone. Hit the target? Delete it (or rename it to "Emergency fund — done" if you want the satisfaction of seeing the full ring before you nuke it).
